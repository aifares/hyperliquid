# Strategy audit — 2026-07-16

Grounded in: today's daily review (18 closed trades), config as deployed, all
backtests (RESULTS.md), wallet research, and the live session record. DB
aggregates unavailable this pass (tooling outage) — per-tier all-time stats

against config/reports directly.

## A. Inconsistencies found

1. **Stale run-up concurrency twin** — `RUNUP_MAX_CONCURRENT=4` (the cap the
   tier actually uses) vs `TIER_MAX_CONCURRENT["runup"]=2` (stale; only feeds
   `guardrail_block("runup")`, which nothing in the run-up path calls — the
   tier sizes via `_slot()` and enters directly). Functionally harmless today,
   but two sources of truth for the same number is how future bugs happen.
2. **Rally: 5% of bankroll allocated to a disabled tier** (`RALLY_ENABLED=0`
   in .env, but `TIER_BUDGET_FRAC["rally"]=0.05`). $2 permanently idle.
3. **Journal now stores smart-money-adjusted conviction, not the raw news
   read** (`log_signal(confidence=eff_conf)`). This destroys the ability to
   later answer "is Claude's conviction calibrated?" and "did the SM weight
   help?" separately — the two effects are fused in the data. Should store
   raw conf + sm_delta separately.
4. **The overnight crypto shadow test is collecting ~nothing**: settle tasks
   are in-memory (`asyncio.sleep(4h)`), and the bot restarted ~7× today —
   every restart kills in-flight settles. shadow_crypto.jsonl stays empty at
   this restart cadence. Entries must be persisted at entry time and settled
   on startup, or the experiment produces no data.
5. **Kill switch can't see the real losses**: `DAILY_LOSS_LIMIT` counts only
   `STOP` exits. Today: −$4.65 realized with ONE stop — the damage came via
   EXTERNAL/NEWS/FADE exits, which never arm the breaker. A −$11 day (7/15)
   also wouldn't trip it. It should be a daily realized-drawdown breaker
   (e.g. halt at −8–10% of bankroll/day), not a stop-counter.
6. **Floor-hugging is back at the swing floor**: fired swings today were conf
   0.65 (SPCX), 0.65 (SP500), 0.75 (XYZ100) — the same at-the-minimum pattern
   that preceded the raised floors on 7/14. The signal distribution clusters
   at the bar; anything admitted at exactly the floor is the weakest allowed.
7. **No correlation/sector cap**: MU, SKHX, SKHY, SNDK, SMSN (+DRAM complex)
   are effectively one memory-cycle trade in five costumes. Run-up (4 slots) +
   swing (2) could stack 6 correlated positions with no rule against it.
   Today's book was repeatedly all-memory.
8. **Residual exit asymmetry (accepted, documented)**: full news-exit bar
   (0.70) still sits above entry (0.65); the PROTECT gear (0.55) covers the
   gap non-destructively. Fine — but worth remembering it's there.
9. **Smart-money weights are invented constants** (+0.05/+0.10/−0.08/−0.15,
   caps +0.12/−0.18) — plausible but uncalibrated, and they now affect real
   sizing. Need the daily review to compare confirmed-vs-unconfirmed outcomes
   and tune (or zero) them with data.
10. **Tracked-wallet staleness risk**: the first MM wallet studied rotated
    addresses and went dormant mid-June. Our 6 tracked wallets will decay the
    same way; the watchlist needs a scheduled re-scan (weekly) rather than a
    frozen list.

## B. Behavioral concerns (from today's 18 trades)

- **EXTERNAL churn is still the #1 cost**: 11/18 exits were EXTERNAL (manual
  closes of bot positions), including −$6.96 NVDA and −$1.88/−$0.55/−$0.37 MU
  re-closes. The bot's wins (+$4.23 FADE, +$3.57 OFFHOURS_DERISK) were real;
  the manual overrides erased them. Decide: bot-managed or hand-managed, not
  both on the same coins.
- **22% daily win rate is churn-distorted, not strategy-distorted** — most
  losers were sub-$1 manual scratches, not thesis failures.
- **OFFHOURS_DERISK worked as designed** (4 flatten-before-close exits, incl.
  +$3.57 NVDA banked before the overnight gap window).
- **Restart frequency is itself a risk factor**: each restart re-arms
  watchers, kills shadow settles, and re-checks run-up windows. Fewer, batched
  config changes from here.

## C. Efficiency/profitability improvements (ranked by value ÷ effort)

1. **Daily drawdown breaker** (replaces stop-count kill switch) — halts all
   entries for the day at −8% of bankroll realized. Cheap, huge tail value.
2. **Sector cap** — max 2 concurrent positions per correlated complex
   (memory: MU/SKHX/SKHY/SNDK/SMSN; AI-infra: NVDA/AMD/MRVL/NBIS; etc.).
3. **Persist shadow settles** so the crypto experiment actually accrues data.
4. **Split raw vs effective conviction in the journal** (calibration hygiene).
5. **Macro-event cooldown** — hardcode the FOMC/CPI/NFP calendar (public,
   free); block new entries ±30min around prints. Complements the index ban —
   single names whipsaw on those prints too (both MU stop-outs today were
   inside the inflation-print window).
6. **Build PEAD as the run-up's second leg** — our own backtest validated it
   (+1.30%/event, 56% win on TSLA/AMD/AAPL/INTC only). Natural capital
   hand-off: run-up exits BEFORE the print, PEAD enters AFTER it — same
   calendar, same names, opposite side of the announcement. Earnings season
   starts next week; this is the highest-value unbuilt validated edge.
7. **Retire the scalp tier** (5% → run-up or swing). Its $2 share can't fund
   a meaningful position and the geometry study never supported it.
8. Align the run-up concurrency twins (cosmetic, 1-line).

## D. Strategy research — what's real, per evidence

Validated by our own backtests: run-up (live), PEAD (validated, UNBUILT),
BTC capitulation-wick fade (marginal; shadow only). Tested and dead: FOMC
drift, funding-extreme fade, BTC→alt lead-lag, 24h momentum, index breakout.

Externally documented, compatible with our stack, worth testing in order:
1. **Insider-cluster buying** (multiple insiders buying within days —
   Cohen/Malloy/Pomorski-style "opportunistic" filter): decades of evidence of
   post-filing drift. Finnhub's free tier exposes insider transactions for
   our names → cheap to wire as a swing-confidence input, backtestable.
2. **Overnight-vs-intraday return split** (equities historically earn their
   return overnight): our perps trade the gap, and we already hold 10y of
   daily bars → a "long near close, exit near open" overlay is backtestable
   this week with data on hand.
3. **Analyst revision momentum** (post-revision drift): Finnhub estimates
   endpoints; moderate effort.
4. **Short-interest squeezes** (FINRA biweekly, via Finnhub): situational,
   pairs well with news triggers on high-SI names (HOOD-type).
5. **Sector pairs** (long strongest / short weakest memory name): directly
   monetizes the correlation problem instead of just capping it; needs its
   own backtest; fee-heavier (two legs).

## E. Extra information that could add real edge (priority order)

1. Macro calendar (FOMC/CPI/NFP dates) — free, hardcodable, immediate value.
2. Finnhub insider transactions — free tier, validated academic edge.
3. Weekly smart-money re-scan (keep the tracked-wallet set fresh).
4. Finnhub earnings estimates/revisions + short interest — cheap adds.
5. L2 depth — still NOT recommended until single-name flow is consistently
   profitable; unbacktestable (no historical book) and thin books here.

## Verdict

The system's bones are good: the validated backbone (run-up) is deployed and
about to get its first full earnings-season test; protections (brackets,
derisk, three-tier news response, trend filter, index ban) all fired correctly
today. The measured leaks, in order: manual/bot churn (largest, behavioral),
no daily drawdown breaker, correlated-position stacking, and a shadow
experiment silently collecting nothing. Fix those four and build PEAD before
adding any new signal source.
